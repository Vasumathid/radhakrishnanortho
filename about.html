15 Years of Orthopaedic Practice

The surgeon who explains "why" before he ever recommends surgery.

Dr. Radhakrishnan built International Ortho Care on one principle: a patient who understands their condition heals with more confidence than one who simply follows instructions.

From SRM to Sri Ramachandra

Dr. Radhakrishnan completed his MBBS at SRM University in 2010, before going on to specialise with an MS in Orthopedics at Sri Ramachandra University, Chennai, in 2016. That six-year stretch between degrees was spent deliberately — gaining hands-on surgical experience before specialising, rather than rushing straight through.

Today he holds Tamil Nadu Medical Council registration 94681, and practices as a joint replacement surgeon, spine surgeon and orthopedist across two clinics: International Ortho Care in Vadapalani, and a second location in Kotturpuram.

A Practice Built on Listening First

Patients who've been to multiple specialists before arriving here often say the same thing: he's the first doctor who actually explained what was happening to their body. One patient's family was told their mother needed back surgery elsewhere — Dr. Radhakrishnan reviewed the case, explained the condition clearly, and resolved it without an operation.

That's not an isolated story. It's the standing instruction at this clinic: recommend the simplest effective treatment, not the most billable one.

Why Same-Day Walking Matters

For joint replacement patients specifically, Dr. Radhakrishnan follows enhanced-recovery surgical protocols designed to get patients standing and taking guided steps within 24 hours of surgery wherever clinically appropriate. Early mobilisation isn't just about comfort — it measurably reduces complications like blood clots and accelerates the return to independent living.

Treatment Philosophy

Three rules this clinic never breaks

01

Explain before you treat

Every diagnosis comes with a plain-language explanation of what's actually happening in the joint, bone or spine — not just a prescription.

02

Surgery is the last option

Medication, injections and physiotherapy are tried first wherever clinically sound. Surgery is recommended only when it's genuinely the better path.

03

Mobilise early, recover faster

For patients who do need joint replacement, early guided movement — often within 24 hours — is built into the recovery plan from day one.

Have a question before you book?
